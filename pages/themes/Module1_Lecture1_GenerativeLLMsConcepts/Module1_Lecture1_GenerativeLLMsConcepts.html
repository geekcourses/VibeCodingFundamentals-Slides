What is Vibe Coding?

Definition and Essence

The Concept

Vibe Coding is a paradigm shift in software development where the focus moves from manual syntax construction to high-level intent and flow management.

It involves "vibing" with the AI—guiding it through natural language to achieve the desired outcome, treating the AI as a capable pair programmer or even a junior developer.

Core Philosophy

  • Intent over Syntax: You define what you want, the AI figures out how.
  • Flow State: Maintaining a continuous creative flow without getting bogged down in boilerplate.
  • Curator Mindset: The developer becomes a reviewer and architect, curating the AI's output.

History and Popularization

Origins

Andrej Karpathy's Influence

The term "Vibe Coding" was significantly popularized by Andrej Karpathy.

"I just want to write the vibe of the code, not the code itself."
https://x.com/karpathy/status/1886192184808149383?lang=en

By 2025, this concept evolved from a meme to a legitimate development methodology, driven by the capabilities of advanced models like GPT-4, Claude 3.5, and Gemini.

The Timeline

  • Pre-2023: Traditional coding & basic autocomplete.
  • 2023-2024: Rise of Copilots and Chat-based coding (AI-Assisted).
  • 2025: Vibe Coding—AI manages entire files and features based on high-level "vibes" or specs.

Understanding LLMs

Under the Hood

How LLMs Work

At their core, Large Language Models are next-token predictors.

They don't "know" facts in the human sense; they understand statistical relationships between words (tokens) based on massive amounts of training data.

Training Pipeline

  1. Pre-training: Learning grammar, facts, and reasoning from the internet (unsupervised).
  2. Fine-tuning (SFT): Learning to follow instructions and answer questions (supervised).
  3. RLHF: Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback to align with human preferences (safety, helpfulness).

Working Effectively with LLMs

  • Context is King: Provide clear, comprehensive context.
  • Iterative Refinement: Don't expect perfection in shot one. Converse and refine.
  • Verification: Always review the code. LLMs can hallucinate (make things up).
  • Prompt Engineering: The skill of clearly articulating the "vibe" or requirement.
